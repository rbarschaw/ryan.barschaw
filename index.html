The Challenge

Microsoft's Partner Center serves 400,000+ partners globally. The organization needed an AI assistant that could actually ship to production — not a demo, but a real product with all the legal, privacy, security, and responsible AI requirements that entails.

My Role

I owned the product end-to-end: partnering with engineering to define the architecture (RAG-based grounding, plugin framework, orchestration layer), designing the phased rollout with Go/No-Go gates, and coordinating 10+ organizations including engineering, data science, legal, privacy, security, marketing, operations, support, field, and sales.

When pressure mounted for an early broad launch, I designed a private preview strategy instead — staged rollout with quality gates that validated the product at scale before going wide. It was the right call: zero safety incidents, and the framework became the org-wide template.

I also built the responsible AI governance framework — compliance processes, safety review gates, and launch readiness criteria — which was adopted as the org-wide template across Commerce + Ecosystems.

Impact

400K+ partners served 8-figure annual cost savings Zero safety incidents RAI framework adopted org-wide

Harmony Portal Re-architecture
Led a full re-architecture of a portal serving millions of monthly visits — from monolithic server-side rendering to a decentralized client-side framework.
Read case study →
+

The Challenge

Partner Center had grown into a monolithic portal with front door load times of 4-5 seconds, painful developer onboarding, and no ability for individual workspace teams to ship independently. With millions of monthly visits and 19 workspaces, the platform needed a fundamental re-architecture without disrupting the existing user base.

My Role

I led the migration from centralized server-side rendering to a decentralized client-side services framework. This meant defining the target architecture, sequencing the rollout across 19 workspaces (each with its own engineering team and priorities), and driving adoption of a shared design system and component library.

The developer experience was equally important: I built tooling that cut onboarding from days to 15 minutes and new page creation from multi-day to 5 minutes. I also built the portal observability platform — scalable telemetry pipelines and product feedback infrastructure used to drive decisions across all workspaces.

Impact

Latency reduced 90%+ 4-5s → sub-second load Dev onboarding: days → 15 min 19 workspaces migrated 40+ languages, 200+ countries

Sovereign Cloud Migrations
Migrated 12,000+ customers from sovereign clouds to global infrastructure with zero downtime and zero partner impact.
Read case study →
+

The Challenge

Microsoft's sovereign cloud offerings (Germany, US Government) needed to migrate customers to global Azure infrastructure. These were regulated environments with strict data residency requirements. Any downtime would impact thousands of customers and their end users — and any compliance misstep could have regulatory consequences.

My Role

This was a pure cross-org coordination challenge. The migration touched identity, commerce, Office, and Azure teams, each with hard dependencies on the others. I designed the migration sequencing to handle those dependencies, built the playbooks and communication templates for each customer cohort, and built the telemetry to track progress and catch issues before they became outages.

The key insight was treating this as a program management problem, not a technical one. The engineering was well-understood — the risk was in the coordination. I built a dependency graph across all four service teams and sequenced cutovers to ensure that no customer was ever in a half-migrated state.

Impact

12,000+ customers migrated Zero downtime Zero partner impact Regulated environments (DE, US Gov)
Perspectives
How I think about the work

Bring the AI to the work, not the work to the AI

We launched an AI assistant to 400K+ partners. Adoption was low. Partners had to leave their task, open a separate panel, and re-describe their situation from scratch. The fix wasn't a better model — it was embedding the AI directly into the workflow with the context already there. Usage went up 10x in the first pilot. The lesson I keep coming back to: placement and context matter more than capability.

Ship the governance with the product

Teams that bolt on responsible AI at the end get blocked at launch. I've seen it happen — legal asks questions nobody planned for, security finds gaps, and the ship date slips. When you build the governance framework alongside the product from day one (safety review gates, launch readiness criteria, escalation paths), you don't slow down. You've already answered every question before it gets asked. That's how you get leadership to say yes.

Your first users are the teams who build on your platform

I've spent most of my career building things that other teams adopt: a portal framework across 19 workspaces, a contextual AI API that every workspace team integrated, a design system that hit 100% adoption. The pattern is always the same — if you make it easy for internal teams to build on what you've made, external adoption follows. Developer onboarding went from days to 15 minutes. That's the multiplier.
